Featured Case

Avoiding €40–45M CAPEX through early-stage technical and commercial validation

Technical Due Diligence

Problem

A proposed long-distance sewage pumping and sub-sea transfer system involved significant capital exposure, with uncertainty across hydraulic design, routing and long-term operational performance.

Response

Led early-stage hydraulic analysis and concept evaluation, combined with techno-commercial assessment of CAPEX and OPEX. Identified key technical constraints and cost drivers affecting feasibility.

Outcome

Revealed that the proposed solution was not economically or operationally viable, avoiding €40–45M high-risk capital commitment.

Moving a technology from pilot to full-scale reference

Technical Due Diligence PMO Support & Integration

Problem

A wastewater technology transitioning from pilot to its first full-scale reference faced unclear integration with existing infrastructure and significant operational uncertainty.

Response

Led technical delivery of the full-scale demonstration, aligning process & mechanical design, R&D, engineering and operations. Established structured root cause analyses and learning cycles to resolve issues and stabilise performance under real conditions.

Outcome

Full-scale reference plant delivered within 8 months. Improved operational stability and reduced uncertainty ahead of further deployment.

Clarifying delivery and cost structure before scale

Business Development Technical Due Diligence

Problem

A water technology provider delivering its first reference project in a new market faced uncertainty around membrane selection, performance and cost structure, with limited visibility into EPC and supplier delivery efficiency.

Response

Undertook a focused early-stage assessment of delivery workflows, supplier interfaces and decision points. Identified coordination gaps, hidden cost drivers and structural inefficiencies across the EPC and supply chain.

Outcome

Improved clarity on membrane and supplier decisions and overall cost structure. Identified opportunities to reduce hidden costs and supply-chain friction ahead of scaling, with potential cost improvements of approximately 25%.
